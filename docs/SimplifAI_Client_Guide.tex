\section{Introduction}

SimplifAI Property Assets is a web application for managing property-related money flows:
owner \textbf{deposits} (income), property \textbf{expenses} (costs), and the checks needed to keep both accurate.

The application is organised around a simple idea: every transaction belongs to a \textbf{property}, and every property belongs to an \textbf{owner}.
The sidebar on the left is the main way to move between sections.

\section{Dashboard}

The \textbf{Dashboard} is the starting point. It answers: ``How are we doing right now?''

\subsection{Period filter}

At the top you can choose \textbf{Month}, \textbf{Quarter}, or \textbf{Year}, and pick the year (and month or quarter anchor).
Most numbers on the dashboard follow this period --- deposits, expenses, gaps, owner totals, and recent activity.

\subsection{Quick actions}

Four shortcut buttons let you jump straight to common tasks:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{Import file} --- upload a spreadsheet or document on the Transactions page
  \item \textbf{Add expense} --- open the manual expense form
  \item \textbf{View alerts} --- open the Alerts page (shows a count if anything is open)
  \item \textbf{Ask AI} --- open the AI Query page
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Summary cards}

The top row of cards shows, for the selected period:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item Total \textbf{deposits} and how many deposit transactions
  \item Total \textbf{expenses} and how many expense transactions
  \item \textbf{Net position} (deposits minus expenses)
  \item Total \textbf{transactions}
  \item \textbf{Open alerts} (errors and warnings)
  \item \textbf{Missing deposits} --- expected owner deposits not found in the period
\end{itemize}

\screenshot{screenshots/02-dashboard-top.png}{Dashboard summary cards and period filter}{%
  Dashboard top section: period selector, quick actions, and summary cards.%
}

\subsection{Pending uploads and alerts at a glance}

Two panels side by side:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{Pending uploads} --- files that were uploaded and analysed but not yet confirmed
  \item \textbf{Alerts at a glance} --- the most important open alerts; click any row to go to Alerts
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Property health}

Each property appears as a card showing deposits, expenses, net, and a \textbf{deposit status} badge:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{On track} --- the expected monthly deposit was found for the period
  \item \textbf{Partial} --- some months in the period are missing (quarter/year view)
  \item \textbf{Missing} --- the expected deposit was not found
\end{itemize}

\noindent
\textit{Note:} Deposit status compares \textbf{expected monthly amounts} (configured per property) against \textbf{actual deposit records} in the system.
It does \textbf{not} depend on whether a file was uploaded --- only on confirmed deposit entries.

\screenshot{screenshots/03-dashboard-property-health.png}{Property health cards}{%
  Property health cards showing deposits, expenses, net, and deposit status.%
}

\subsection{Other dashboard sections}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Expense breakdown} --- bar view of spending by category (utilities, maintenance, tax, etc.)
  \item \textbf{Missing expected deposits} --- table of properties where the monthly deposit was not received
  \item \textbf{Owner overview} --- per-owner deposits, expenses, and net for the period
  \item \textbf{Recent activity} --- latest deposits and expenses in the selected period
\end{itemize}

\section{Properties}

The \textbf{Properties} page lists every managed property.

For each property you typically see:

\section{Transactions}
\label{sec:transactions}

The \textbf{Transactions} page is where day-to-day financial records live.
Deposits and expenses appear together; deposits are highlighted in \textbf{green}, expenses in \textbf{red}.

\subsection{Filter tabs}

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{All} --- deposits and expenses combined, sorted by date
  \item \textbf{Deposits} --- income only
  \item \textbf{Expenses} --- costs only
\end{itemize}

Additional filters: property, owner, date range, and (for expenses) category and source.

\subsection{Summary cards}

Four cards at the top show total deposits, total expenses, net, and how many rows match the current filters.

\subsection{Add expense (manual)}

Click \textbf{Add expense} to enter a single expense by hand:
property, date, amount, category, source, payment method, and optional description.

\screenshot{screenshots/07-transactions-add-expense.png}{Manual expense form}{%
  Transactions page with the manual expense form open.%
}

\subsection{Import from file}

Click \textbf{Import from file} to upload:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item Excel or CSV spreadsheets
  \item PDF documents
  \item Images (receipts, invoices)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Steps:}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Choose the \textbf{property} and \textbf{transaction type} (deposit or expense)
  \item Select the file
  \item Click \textbf{Analyze file} --- the system reads the file and proposes rows to save
  \item Review the table: yellow warnings mean ``please verify''; red means a field must be fixed
  \item Click \textbf{Confirm} to save approved rows
\end{enumerate}

\screenshot{screenshots/08-transactions-import.png}{File import and review step}{%
  Import panel: file selection, analyse step, and review table before confirming.%
}

\section{Alerts}

The \textbf{Alerts} page collects items that need attention.

\subsection{Types of alerts}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Missing deposit} --- expected monthly deposit not found; you can add the deposit and resolve the alert
  \item \textbf{Upload review} --- a file was uploaded but not yet confirmed
  \item \textbf{Possible duplicate} --- a deposit may already exist; you can skip or confirm
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Working with an alert}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Click an alert in the list on the left
  \item The fix panel on the right shows what to do:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item For missing deposits: fill in bank account, date, and amount, then save
    \item For uploads: edit extracted rows, skip duplicates if needed, then confirm
  \end{itemize}
  \item Alternatively, click \textbf{Dismiss} if the alert does not require action
\end{enumerate}

The Alerts menu item shows a \textbf{red count badge} when open alerts exist.

\screenshot{screenshots/10-alerts.png}{Alerts list and resolve panel}{%
  Alerts page: alert list on the left, resolution form on the right.%
}

\section{AI Query}

The \textbf{AI Query} page lets you ask questions about your data in natural language, for example:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item ``Total deposits for Rothschild in Q1 2026''
  \item ``Which properties had no deposit in March 2026?''
  \item ``Show electricity expenses for Dizengoff''
\end{itemize}

The answer appears as readable text plus a results table when applicable.
You can export results to Excel from this page.

Example prompts are shown on the page to help you get started.

\section{Typical workflows}

\subsection{Recording a new month of owner deposits}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Prepare an Excel file with deposit rows (see Section~\ref{sec:transactions})
  \item Go to \textbf{Transactions} $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Import from file}
  \item Choose type \textbf{Deposit}, select the file, analyse, and confirm
  \item Check the \textbf{Dashboard} --- property health should show \textbf{On track} for properties that received their expected deposit
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Recording property expenses}

Either:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item Use \textbf{Add expense} for a single manual entry, or
  \item Use \textbf{Import from file} with type \textbf{Expense} (one upload per property)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Clearing alerts}

Open \textbf{Alerts}, work through each item, then confirm the dashboard alert count drops to zero.

\section{Current scope and next steps}

This prototype focuses on:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item Viewing and filtering deposits and expenses
  \item Importing data from files with a human review step
  \item Detecting missing expected deposits
  \item A central alerts workflow
  \item Natural-language queries over financial data
\end{itemize}

\noindent
After the client meeting, feedback may lead to new requirements --- additional reports, document storage, notifications, user roles, Hebrew interface, and other items discussed in the full product vision.
